South America.

In South America, there are a variety of traditional festivals that reflect the brilliance of traditional culture. For example, Peru's "Sun Festival." Peru is the birthplace of the Incan civilization, one of the three oldest civilizations in the Americas. The ancient Incas claimed to be the "sons of the sun," and the sun was regarded as the highest deity of nature. Therefore, in the traditional Peruvian festival, the solar festival in June of each year Undoubtedly the most important. In addition, the "corn festival" in Venezuela. The Caribbean country Venezuela is also inhabited by many Indians and still retains unique traditional customs. Compared with Peru, there is a bit more passion in the Caribbean. The Indians in southern Venezuela celebrate “corn festival” every year on September 24, because corn is a traditional crop in the region. People here eat corn, drink corn juice, and weave with corn stalks, so they have deep feelings for corn. . Also, Bolivia's "God of Wealth." On January 24 of each year, Bolivia's "God of Wealth Festival", God of Wealth wears traditional costumes and holds a lot of money in his hands. It is said that as long as the model of the thing you want is placed in the hands of the God of Wealth dolls, and then burn the doll after wishing, you can get what you want. The Brazilian Carnival is the largest carnival in the world and is known as the "Greatest Show on Earth." Three days a year or mid-February.
