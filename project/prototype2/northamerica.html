North America.

North American culture is very rich. North America is a melting pot of nations and a place where the world's cultures converge. For example, New Year's Day, Lincoln's Birthday, Valentine's Day, Washington's Birthday, April Fools' Day, Mothers' Day, Labor Day, Columbus Day, Veteran's Day, Hallowmas, Thanksgiving Day and Christmas. The most typical decoration in Christmas is a Christmas tree. People will hang gifts and lanterns on small fir trees or small pine trees, and then put a big star on the top of trees. These decorations are all symbolic. The lanterns on the tree symbolize that Jesus brings light to the world. The big star on the treetop represents the star that was brought to Bethlehem by the three Eastern Magi after Jesus was born. In addition to the Christmas tree, holly and mistletoe are also common decorations in Christmas. Americans often use holly twigs to hang wreaths on the gate, or put a few holly on the table as a festive decoration.
